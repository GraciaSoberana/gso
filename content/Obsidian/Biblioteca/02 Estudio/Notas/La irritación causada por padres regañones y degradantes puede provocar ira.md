> Logically, the irritation caused by nagging and demeaning fathers in the context of everyday life may in turn cause children to become angry. This anger grows, no doubt, out of the frustration of never being able to please fathers who constantly nag or demean them. The personal pronoun ὑμῶν indicates that these children belong to the fathers and they are responsible for them.
> <br />
> <span class="author">Horold Hoerner</span><br ><span class="book">Ephesians, An exegetical commentary</span>

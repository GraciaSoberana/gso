## I. The Holy Spirit’s Ongoing New Covenant Work

 >**The New Covenant Work of the Holy Spirit**: to exalt Jesus Christ by (a) making Him glorious in our eyes, (b) making His saving work effective in our lives, and (c) making His presence in and among us an experienced and operative reality.

b.     “Filled with/by the Spirit” – Acts 4:8, 31; 6:3, 5; 11:24; 13:9; Ephesians 5:18

(1)   Being “filled with the Spirit” often indicates an ongoing quality of life and character in which one is actively dependent upon God and consistently under the influence of the Holy Spirit (Acts 6:3, 5; 11:24; Eph 5:18).

>En el centro de la pneumatologia esta la dependencia


Ephesians 5:18: the one text that commands us to be “filled with the Holy Spirit”

Ø  “To allow oneself to be under the control of the Holy Spirit”

Ø  “To allow oneself to be changed more and more into the image of God and Christ”—i.e., we’re not filled “with” the Spirit as content, but “by” the Spirit as the agent, who fills us with “the fullness of God” (1:23) or Christ (4:13).

> These interpretations complement each other: to be “filled to the full” by the Spirit will indeed result in Christ’ own character being more manifest in our lives.

 A suggested application: Psalm 25:1: “To you, O Lord, I lift up my soul.”

> When I pray this, I am seeking to make Christ the chief object of my affections, making His glory the chief goal of my actions, and making His all-sufficiency the chief object of my dependence and well-spring of my hope.

### E. The Holy Spirit **_Empowers_**

#### 1.     Power for Life and Ministry: “Filling” (Acts 4:8; 30; 13:9; cf. Acts 6:5, 8 with 6:10)

> Being “filled with the Spirit” can also indicate an instantaneous intensification of the Spirit’s presence that provides (a) empowerment for an act of ministry or (b) strength for obedience and godly resolve (Acts 4:8, 31; 13:9; poss. Eph 5:18).

> Positioning ourselves to _receive_ an “instantaneous” filling should not be an “instantaneous” event. ==We position ourselves for such sovereign empowerments by seeking to be _constantly_ “filled with the Spirit”—through, e.g.: a consistent drawing near to the Lord, seeking his presence and delighting in him; daily dependence upon the Lord, seeking his grace, guidance, & glory; a consistent intake and internalization of his Word, studying it, embracing it, and meditating upon it; and accessing all the means of grace available to us individually and in the church.==

> “If I want to be filled with fullness of the Spirit, then let me set my heart on doing the will of God and call upon him for the enablement to do so.”
> \- Graham Cole

Ø  SGC Statement of Faith

**The Filling of the Spirit**

When Christ ascended, he poured out the Holy Spirit on the church, ushering in a greater experience of God’s presence and power among his people. The Spirit transforms hearts by the miracle of regeneration and indwells all believers in abundant, new covenant measure. The Spirit also desires to fill God’s people continually with increased power for Christian life and witness. To be filled with the Spirit is to be more fully under his influence, more aware of his presence, and more effective in his service. All Christians, therefore, must continually seek to be filled with the Spirit by living and praying in such a way that invites the Spirit’s work among us, actively longing for God to accomplish his gracious purposes in us and through us. The filling of the Spirit brings to God’s people a deeper knowledge of Christ, an increased desire for holiness, a stronger commitment to unity and love, a greater fruitfulness in ministry, and a deeper gratitude for our salvation.

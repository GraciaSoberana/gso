> One of the important events in the history of Pergamon was the defeat of the Celtic tribes in 230 B.C., and it was in commemoration of this event that the great altar of Zeus was constructed. Sacrificial victims were burned twenty-four hours a day, seven days each week by a rotating group of priests (Metzger, Code, 34).
